Ages 18 Months to 3 Years

Stepping Stones

Dance With Me

Introduce your child to the wonders of music and movement. Our Mommy (Daddy) and Me class offers a time for the parent and child to learn together. This class provides a fun creative opportunity for your child to interact with other children. The class will work towards developing motor skills, hand/eye coordination, creativity and imagination. The young child will have the security of their parent taking part in the class, and not have the separation anxiety experienced by some children.

Watch Me Dance

Watch Me Dance is the next step for little dancers ready to take class on their own. Students build independence, confidence, and classroom skills while parents watch from the viewing area. Classes focus on music, movement, coordination, and social interaction in a nurturing, age-appropriate environment.

Ages 3 to 4 — Beginner

Creative Dance

This is a 30 minute class geared at introducing ballet and jazz techniques to young dancers. This class helps the young student become acclimated to new environments and teaches them coordination and the fundamentals needed to advance.

Preschool Classes — Ages 4 to 5

Tap and Twirl

This class is a 30 minute class with the time split between the tap and jazz genres. These combo classes give the young dancer the basics of each genre in a fun and creative manner.

Ages 4 to 6

Kinder Classes

Jazz/Tap

Combo classes split between tap and jazz give young dancers the basics of each genre in a fun, creative way. Students build coordination, rhythm, and confidence while learning foundational steps in both styles.

Ballet

Ballet introduces dancers to important foundation skills in a fun, age-appropriate setting. Classes develop posture, body placement, and grace while keeping young students engaged with music and props.

Hip Hop

Hip hop classes for kinder dancers focus on high-energy movement, rhythm, and self-expression. Students learn basic hip hop steps and combinations in a supportive, upbeat environment.

Beginner Acro / Tumbling

Introduces flexibility, strength, balance, and basic tumbling skills in a safe, beginner-friendly format. Students build body control and confidence through age-appropriate acrobatic exercises.

Ages 8 through Teen

Primary & Teen Classes

Ballet

Ballet is based on techniques that have been developed over centuries and serves as a back bone for many other styles of dance. Ballet has a specific training curriculum to develop good posture, body placement, alignment, balance, strength and muscle control. It is an elegant and graceful dance art and the foundation of all dance.

Jazz

Jazz is available for students 6 years and older with more serious studies for ages 8 through teen. Jazz is a highly energized and rhythmic style of dance that was born in America. Jazz technique involves sharp isolated movements combined with coordination, flexibility and strength. The influence of musical styles past and present, current social & cultural influences, all make up the energy of jazz.

Tap

The tap program is based on a graded system of tap for all ages. Beginning with a primary tap program ages 7 to 9, with more serious studies beginning at age 8 to teen. Specific tap steps and progressions must be acquired for each level of training. This is a great class to tap out rhythms and master the sounds from your feet to the music.

Acro / Tumbling

Teaches flexibility, strength, balance, muscle control and tumbling skills, as well as technical precision, discipline and concentration. Students are taught both power tumbling and acrobatic strength and flexibility tricks. Available for ages 6 and older.

Lyrical / Contemporary (Intermediate/Advanced Dancers only)

These classes are offered to experienced ballet students. Lyrical is an advanced style of dance that borrows the techniques of ballet, jazz and modern. The quality of movement is closely connected to the quality of the music and the interpretation of the lyrics to convey the emotion or story of the song. Contemporary is a style of expressive dance that combines elements of several dance genres including modern, jazz, lyrical and classical ballet. Contemporary dance can be performed to many different styles of music.
