Dancer Hair — High Pony

The dancers in the routines listed below will wear their hair in a slicked-back high ponytail. All baby hairs and bangs must be bobby pinned or gelled back (no hair on the forehead). The pony needs to be high on the head, not at the back of the head. Curls in the ponytail are optional, but look adorable on stage!

  • My Teddy Bear (Monday 4:05 Tutu Cute)
  • The Coral Reef Song (Monday 4:45 Pre Ballet/Jazz)
  • Pretty Little Baby (Monday 6:30 Acro)
  • Let's Be Super Heroes (Tuesday 5:30 Pre Ballet/Jazz)
  • When You Wish Upon a Star (Tuesday 6:00 Kinder Ballet)
  • Girl in the Mirror / Broadway Baby (Tuesday 6:30 Dance Fusion)

Dancer Hair — Low Bun

Dancers in all other routines will wear their hair in a bun as described below:

  • Wear their hair in a low center bun with a center part.
  • The hair should be slicked down/back with no bangs or baby hairs (no hair on the forehead).
  • Bun nets are REQUIRED for all dancers wearing a bun.
  • A bun form/donut is an easy way to achieve a nice bun!

Makeup

We ask that all dancers wear makeup at the recital, as the stage lights will wash them out. Please follow the guidelines below:

  • Makeup should be natural tones (no blues, greens, or purples!). Blush is a must for all ages.
  • Dancers age 3 to 5: please use blush and light eyeshadow. Lipstick/gloss is good too!
  • Elementary and older dancers: if you need guidance with makeup, you can reference a tutorial as a guide. You do not need expensive brands — the same look can be recreated with less costly products from Target, Walmart, etc. Fake lashes are NOT necessary!
  • Nails: dancers can NOT have nails that are bright/bold/glittery. The only acceptable polish is clear or very light nude. No acrylic or long nails — it's not safe when dancing.
